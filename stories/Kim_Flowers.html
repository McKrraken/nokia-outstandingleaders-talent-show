Kim Flowers


Coming out is a scary process filled with unknowns and what ifs. I have spent all my work life in the closet until just recently, 2021 I came out. Why now after 30 some years?
For me at least, after being a cohort in the Outstanding Leaders program, the working environment at Nokia seemed to change, it felt more accepting and genuine to me.
I could see other brave souls at Nokia, LGTB+, sharing their personal identities in the workplace without repercussions. Being a cohort gave me the opportunity and a safe place at work to share my real identity.
My cohorts on my project were the 1st colleagues I nervously came out by sharing my pronouns. The visibility to others (like me) coupled with Nokia support policies (such as gender transition at work and healthcare benefits) and support from Nokia Business Services support (allies) such as US in-country benefit support (like Brenda Sitton) and Chief People Office Diversity Equity & Inclusion (like Anneli Karlstedt and Burcu Donmez) gave me the courage to live my identity at work.
Now over time, it's the simple things that make a difference in your life. Like when I received my performance review from my line manager, he didn't miss a beat, he used my proper pronouns, and it made me truly feel accepted and affirmed.
