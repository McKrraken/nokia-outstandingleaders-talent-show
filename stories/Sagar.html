I never imagined that choosing to be true to myself would also mean choosing between staying in the place I was born and venturing to a distant continent. The land of my birth does not offer a society that fully comprehends homonormativity, and from a young age, I longed to be surrounded by people who understood and embraced my true identity.
While I now see significant progress in my country in terms of awareness and acceptance, many of my peers still seek to move abroad, hoping for legal marriage, adoption rights, and the freedom to live authenticallyâ€”opportunities that remain limited here. But what about my roots? The culture, traditions, food, music, films, and art? The sense of completeness I feel when surrounded by my family? Why should I have to abandon all of this in order to be myself? Why must I choose one and lose the other?
I have now made my choice: to stay connected to my roots while embracing my identity and sexuality. I am determined to live as my true self, even if it disrupts the traditions around me, because I know no other way.
